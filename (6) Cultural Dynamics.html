"Social Media and Cultural Dynamics: The Impact of Online Platforms on Norms, Values, and Behaviours"


Shaping Body Image and Beauty Standards


The impact of social media on body image and beauty standards is a growing concern, as it can lead to negative self-perception and self-esteem, and even the development of disordered eating behaviours. Studies have shown that social media can perpetuate narrow, culturally specific beauty standards, which can marginalize those who do not fit these expectations. For instance, research has investigated the influence of social media on body image dissatisfaction among women in Lebanon and how it influences their self-confidence and decision-making. Similarly, social media has been found to intensify body image disorders among both young women and men, because of negative upward comparison of their external appearance. However, there are also positive aspects of social media, such as the online fat acceptance movement, which has become a popular and important space for fat women and their allies to contest the gendered anti-fat discourses perpetuated by the media, governments, and institutions of public health. Overall, it is important to raise awareness of the impact of social media on body image and beauty standards, and to encourage young females and other marginalized groups to understand and reject unrealistic societal beauty ideals trending on social media platforms.

Shaping Gender Roles


Social media has the power to both challenge and reinforce traditional gender roles. While platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter offer spaces for individuals to question, resist, and subvert conventional gender norms, they can also perpetuate harmful stereotypes and expectations, reinforcing traditional gender roles and limiting opportunities for self-expression and personal growth. The impact of social media on gender roles is complex and multifaceted, and it depends on various factors such as the type of media, age, and gender. For instance, a study found that social media exposure tends to significantly influence risk perception, fear, and anger towards COVID-19, which varies across gender. Therefore, it is important to be aware of the potential impact of social media on gender roles and to actively work towards creating more inclusive and diverse representations of gender on these platforms. By doing so, we can foster a more equitable and just society that values and celebrates the diversity of human experience.





1. Janavi, Elmira, Maryam Soleimani, Abbas Gholampour, Mike Friedrichsen and Pejman Ebrahimi. “Effect of Social Media Adoption and Media Needs on Online Purchase Behavior: The Moderator Roles of Media Type, Gender, Age.” (2021).

2. Friedman, Scott, Sonja Schmer-Galunder, Anthony Chen, Robert P. Goldman and Michelle C. Ausman. “Gender Gaps Correlate with Gender Bias in Social Media Word Embeddings.” Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society (2020).




Shaping Social Activism





Social media has become a critical tool as well for social activism, enabling grassroots movements to organize, mobilize, and raise awareness on a wide range of issues. Platforms like Twitter and Facebook have played a pivotal role in the success of movements such as #BlackLivesMatter, #MeToo, and #ClimateStrike. However, the nature of social media can also lead to superficial engagement or "slacktivism," where individuals participate in online activism without taking meaningful action in the real world. Despite this, social media has been shown to be an effective tool for building collective narratives, disseminating movement information, and mobilizing pro-democracy collective action frames.

Echo Chambers, Filter Bubbles and Polarization


Revolutionizing the way we consume information, social media has also created echo chambers and filter bubbles that can contribute to the polarization of society. Echo chambers and filter bubbles refer to the phenomenon where social media algorithms show users content that aligns with their existing interests and beliefs, leading to exposure predominantly to like-minded individuals and content that reinforces their pre-existing opinions. This can limit users' exposure to diverse viewpoints and encourage the adoption of more extreme ideological positions, ultimately contributing to the polarization of society. For instance, the spread of LGBT culture on social media has affected the contemporary overall culture to a certain extent, and the popularity of LGBT culture may not break the public's stereotype of LGBT, but add interesting and fashionable labels to the impression of LGBT, so as to form a new stereotype.





Research has also shown that social media algorithms and users' online social networks may combine to shape user behavior, leading to changes in both diversity and slant of users' information sources. Increased use of Facebook has been associated with increased information source diversity and a shift toward more partisan sites in news consumption, while increased use of Reddit has been associated with increased diversity and a shift toward more moderate sites. Increased use of Twitter, on the other hand, has been associated with little to no change in news consumption.



Echo chambers and filter bubbles on social media can also contribute to societal polarization by limiting individuals' exposure to diverse viewpoints and encouraging the adoption of more extreme ideological positions. These phenomena can manifest in various cultural domains, including politics, religion, and social issues, ultimately hindering the development of shared values and social cohesion. The algorithms used by social media platforms and users' online social networks can combine to shape user behavior, resulting in changes in both diversity and slant of users' information sources.

In conclusion, social media has a profound impact on cultural dynamics, influencing norms, values, and behaviors in complex and often contradictory ways. Social media platforms can both challenge and reinforce traditional attitudes and beliefs, shape social activism, and contribute to the formation of echo chambers, filter bubbles, and societal polarization. Understanding the intricate relationship between social media and cultural dynamics is essential for developing strategies to promote healthy online behaviors and foster a more inclusive, diverse, and interconnected society.
