“Social Media and Mental Health: Unravelling the Connection between Online Behaviour and Well-being”


Symptoms of social media addiction can vary, but some common signs include spending excessive amounts of time on social media, feeling anxious or irritable when unable to access social media, neglecting other responsibilities or activities in favor of social media use, and experiencing negative consequences in personal or professional relationships due to social media use. Other symptoms may include feeling a need to constantly check notifications or updates, experiencing a sense of euphoria or pleasure when using social media, and feeling a loss of control over social media use.

Depression and Anxiety


Several studies have identified a link between excessive social media use and increased levels of depression and anxiety. One possible explanation for this connection is the phenomenon of social comparison. Social media platforms often present idealized versions of people's lives, leading users to compare themselves to others and feel inadequate in the process. This constant comparison can foster feelings of envy, dissatisfaction, and low self-esteem, potentially contributing to the development of depression and anxiety.





However, there is some debate over the relationship between social media use and mental health. A study by Orben et al. analyzed social media use among more than 12,000 teenagers in the United Kingdom over the course of 8 years and found virtually no effect of social media use on life satisfaction either between individuals or in the same individual across time. Another study found that the relationship between social media use and mental health is complex and difficult to follow. While social media can create a sense of community for the user, excessive and increased use of social media, particularly among those who are vulnerable, is correlated with depression and other mental health disorders.


Overall, while the relationship between social media use and mental health is not fully understood, excessive social media use can contribute to the development of depression and anxiety through the phenomenon of social comparison. It is important to be mindful of one's social media use and to take breaks when necessary to prioritize mental health.

Body Image Concerns


Another concern is that social media platforms have become a breeding ground for images of "perfect" bodies and lifestyles, leading to negative body image and dissatisfaction with one's own appearance. Research has shown that individuals who spend more time on social media are more likely to experience body image concerns and, in some cases, develop disordered eating behaviours. The use of social media is not homogeneous, and not all users are exposed to the same content. However, if an individual follows physical appearance-based accounts on social networking platforms, their home pages will be filled with idealized representations of a human body, leading to more intense and frequent comparisons with other people. This unmistakably leads to negative body image, and the solution that stands out is education in media literacy which should develop critical and analytical skills in people. The widespread use of social media in teenagers and young adults could increase body dissatisfaction as well as their drive for thinness, therefore rendering them more vulnerable to eating disorders. It is important to note that the level of education was a confounding factor in the relationship between the frequency of comparing one's own physical appearance to that of people followed on social media and body dissatisfaction and drive for thinness. Therefore, it is crucial to extend research on social media and body image beyond convenience sampling of young, White women and include children and older adults, boys and men, and underrepresented groups. In conclusion, social media platforms have a significant impact on people's emotional states, and it is important to develop critical and analytical skills in people to prevent negative body image and disordered eating behaviours.

Self-Esteem and Self-Worth


In addition, social media use can have a negative impact on self-esteem and self-worth, especially among young people. Studies have shown that social media can lead to body dissatisfaction, eating disorders, and lower self-esteem. The constant need for validation through likes and comments can create an unhealthy dependency on external sources of approval, leading to a decrease in self-esteem and an increase in feelings of worthlessness. Furthermore, social media can foster a sense of FOMO (fear of missing out), which may exacerbate feelings of inadequacy and contribute to lower self-worth. Research has also shown that social media can lead to upward social comparisons, where individuals compare themselves to others who appear to be doing better than them, leading to lower self-esteem. One study found that spending one hour on Facebook daily can result in a 5.574 decrease in an individual's self-esteem score. However, some studies have also found that social media can have positive effects on self-esteem, such as promoting body positivity and social media literacy. Overall, it is important to include limiting social media use, focusing on positive content, and building self-esteem through other means such as exercise, hobbies, and social support.

Personal Identity Development





The pressure to present a curated, idealized version of oneself on social media can lead to a fragmented sense of self and an inability to develop an authentic personal identity. According to a survey of 208 Facebook users, consumers with weak levels of personal identity use social media to increase their identity and popularity, while consumers with strong levels of personal identity use social media for self-expression. This struggle to define oneself can contribute to feelings of confusion, anxiety, and depression. Moreover, social media can have both positive and negative impacts on society. For instance, it can motivate people to do something good in their careers and preserve their identity. However, it can also lead to cybercrime, hacking personal information and security, destroy reputation, sexual harassment, bullying, disinformation, misinformation, and rise of fake news, imitation of foreign culture, addiction in school children and society youths local to global level. Therefore, it is important to understand the impact of social media on personal identity and intimacy levels. Social media users should be aware of the social attributes that cause privacy implications and build profiles that respect their privacy. By understanding the impact of social media on personal identity, we can help young people develop a healthy sense of self and use social media in a positive way.

Cyberbullying and Online Harassment


Becoming more prevalent with the increased use of social media, cyberbullying is a form of bullying or harassment that takes place in the virtual world, using digital devices like computers and cell phones. Victims of cyberbullying often experience increased levels of anxiety, depression, and even suicidal ideation. The impact of cyberbullying on mental health is a pervasive issue, particularly among adolescents. Cyberbullying can have an adverse social effect on its victim, such as social isolation from friends and peers in real life. The anonymity afforded by social media can embolden bullies, making it more difficult for victims to escape the abuse and find support. The victims of cyberbullying can present with social anxiety, depression, and suicidal thoughts, among many others. The risk of exposure to cyberbullying has risen during the pandemic, as young people spend more time on the internet and social media. There is a need for appropriate prevention and intervention strategies to be more effective in addressing cyberbullying. Psychoeducation of adolescents and young adults about the negative impact of cyberbullying on mental health is crucial. The impact of cyberbullying on mental health is a complex issue, and more research is needed to understand the possible mechanisms and mediating factors involved in this relationship.

Although social media has provided a platform for communication, self-expression, and information-sharing, the pervasive use of social media has also given rise to concerns about its impact on mental health. While some studies report no detrimental effects, others suggest a negative impact on mental health, including depression, anxiety, body image concerns, self-esteem, self-worth, and personal identity development. The potential negative impact calls for increased awareness, education, and support to promote healthier online behaviours and mitigate the potential harms associated with social media use.
